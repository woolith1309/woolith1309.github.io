KOL Economy:

The rise of male Consumption.

What KOL is

KOL, better known as Key Opinion Leaders, are what consumers are gravitating towards in hopes to filter the clutter that digital advertising has created.

Key Opinion Leaders allow brands to effectively allocate their marketing budget because of the targeted, niche audience the influencer has access to.

According to a study by McKinsey, KOLs, which is trusted and sought after by consumers, has twice the communication and word-of-mouth benefits as paid advertising, and its retention rate is 37% higher than other ways.

Trendency Analysis

In 2017, 23% of men consumed more than 5000 yuan, while only 15% of women consumed more than 5000 yuan. And as for the luxury consumption, the unit price of male customers is 6% higher than that of female customers.
Male consumption begin to rise.

Consumption driven by influencers

On February 20th 2018, CBNData and Alibaba have jointly released the “ 2017 iFashion consumption trend” based on the Big Data collected by Alibaba. They found that young consumers prefer to follow influencers.Pure product presentation no longer provides a compelling stimulus for consumers to buy. Meanwhile, popular celebrities, KOLs, UGC from peers and PGC from designers has become a strong driver for consumers to purchase. Consumers tend to trust their identity presentation and sense of taste. They tend to imitate them and buy similar products.

KOL Influence

Apart from celebrities, more and more KOLs (Key Opinion Leaders) are influencing the fashion industry. According to CBNData’s report, KOLs can convert lower budgets into higher traffic generation with a strong fan effect, increasing their sales conversion rate. Moreover, vertical industry KOLs are increasing. Consumers tend to favour KOLs with a specialisation. For example, wild-eyed Loli has established her sporty KOL image on Weibo, which help her increase her revenue on Taobao over 90% for sportswear.

New Channel

Greater collaboration between streaming and KOLs hit the fashion industry in recent years. Compared with traditionally displaying the product picture on the website, in this model, more content can be expected. KOLs normally fully introduce the products with script offered by the brand officials and some of their own experiences after using. According to CBNData, High interactivity is an outstanding feature of live broadcasting, with the interaction rate often reaching 20%-30%. At the present stage, this interactive selling mode with strong publicity and favourable prices gives consumers huge attraction.

Interviews

In order to get professional opinions and typical daily real case, we interviewed a 20-year-old KOL driven male consumer and a professor from the school of communication,HKBU.

Leo LI,a KOL driven consumer

“As for the individual buying behavior influenced by KOL, Leo LI, a male year 4 student from Hong Kong Baptist University answered this question. Mr. LI tends to focus on alcoholic drink bloggers and photography bloggers. Compared to listening to shopping assistant in the shopping mall, he prefers the recommendations from the KOl’s videos. He also mentioned that he could realize KOL’s promotion on products, but he would not feel bored or abhorrent facing their promotion video. He would purchase the product only when he actually had a need to satisfy and he thought himself as a rational consumer. “I think what I have bought before from KOL’s recommendation such as camera roll and fruit beer fit my imagination. This may due to my personal affection for wine and photography, and also my careful study and observation towards KOL’s evaluation,” Leo said. However, Mr.LI also had experience of impulse buying. Once he watched a KOL’s video on promoting a special clothing deodorant, he felt novel and after he found the price is low enough, he decided to buy one immediately. “Although I bought it without consideration, it is as useful as what the KOl said in the video. I am satisfied,” he said. ”

Dr Melannie Zhang

“While for such ridiculous mainland market phenomenon, Dr Melannie Zhang, from Hong Kong Baptist University, pointed out that it is resulted from the shift in male aesthetics. “We can see there are many popular male stars famous for their delicate faces from 2016 to 2019. They are called as ‘fresh meat’,” she said. Ms. Zhang thought that the popularity of exquisite male stars in entertainment industry has released signal that boys can also pay attention to make-up and skin care as girls, “‘Delicacy’ has become synonymous with boys, which is new requirement put on boys by the market.” Apart from the change of aesthetics of Chinese men, she said that the way to get shopping skills by watching Kol videos is in line with the consumption psychology of male consumers. “Male consumers may be embarrassed when shopping mall assistants gave suggestions or recommendations for them. Compared to listening suggestions in the shopping mall, watching Kol’s video at home makes them relaxed,” she said. ”

Future prospects about KOL economics

In the last three years, the number of google searches for ‘influencer marketing’ has grown 1500%. In accordance with Influencer Marketing Hub, the KOL marketing industry is estimated to reach 10 billion by 2020. KOL economy was booming for these few years and there was still a large room for it to further develop as the demands and importance of it becomes larger. It is a new marketing approach not just in China, but on a global scale. The overall CTR in China for display advertisements is only 0.12% and the average CTR for Google Advertisement on the display network across all industries is only 0.46%. To deal with the low CTR numbers, many businesses change their tactics by using KOL marketing.

Talking about the future KOL trend, Zhou mentioned KOL 1.0 version is the grassroot celebrity, 2.0 version is active in Weibo, 3.0 is now active in all social and live streaming platforms. In the future, KOLs in vertical field will rise rapidly and have larger space to grow. Meanwhile, KOL agencies will search widely for top KOLs, and there will be a team analyzing KOL positioning and engaging with brands to amplify the KOL effects. A more powerful KOL is not a real person but a virtual IP.

Feelings from customers after buying KOL promotion products

KOLs have established trust with their audience, they can be more convincing than traditional advertisements or branded content. A Mediakix survey conducted at the end of 2018 found that 49% of consumers depend on KOLs’ recommendations for their purchase decisions. The numbers are even higheFr for Millennials and those in Gen-Z!

According to the survey done by Hong Kong Baptist University, around 70% of teenage respondents said that they did purchase any KOL promotion products, however, they discovered that the quality of these goods were not as good as what the KOL stated, especially for the food. Over 30% of respondents agreed with the main job for KOL is selling the goods within their blog, videos or posts, therefore they may not only sell the products in a good quality, but they hope that the KOL could declare the advertisement parts in their social network. Some experts described this situation is related to the relationship between KOL and supporters and it would directly affect the income from advertisement, maybe it is because the feelings that everyone has towards the products or services are varied. They suggested that customers should consider more information and opinions before buying anything.
